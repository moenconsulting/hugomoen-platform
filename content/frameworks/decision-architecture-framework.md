# Decision Architecture Framework

## Status

Draft

## What is Decision Architecture?

Decision Architecture Framework is a framework for improving how organizations make decisions under uncertainty.

The framework is based on a simple observation:

Most organizations do not primarily have a technology problem.

They have a decision problem.

The quality of an organization is largely determined by the quality of its decisions, how those decisions are made, and how effectively the organization learns from their consequences.

Decision Architecture focuses on designing an organization's ability to make, challenge, document and improve decisions over time.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of the framework is to help organizations:

- Make decisions at the right level
- Increase speed without losing direction
- Improve decision quality
- Create stronger decision foundations
- Document important decisions
- Learn from outcomes
- Adapt quickly when circumstances change

The goal is not perfect decisions.

The goal is continuous learning and improvement.

---

## Why Decision Architecture?

Organizations spend significant effort on:

- Organizational design
- Governance models
- Processes
- Technology
- Roles and responsibilities

Despite these investments many organizations continue to struggle with:

- Slow decision-making
- Repeated discussions
- Unclear accountability
- Excessive escalation
- Loss of organizational learning

This framework explores the hypothesis that many of these challenges are fundamentally decision-making challenges.

---

## Core Hypothesis

Good organizations are not defined by perfect decisions.

They are defined by their ability to:

- Establish shared context
- Evaluate alternatives
- Challenge assumptions
- Learn quickly
- Adapt continuously

The objective is not to eliminate mistakes.

The objective is to build organizations that learn faster than the consequences of their mistakes develop.

---

## Seven Principles

The framework currently consists of seven principles:

### 1. Context Before Conclusion

Establish shared understanding before discussing solutions.

### 2. Make Alternatives Explicit

A decision without alternatives is rarely a true decision.

### 3. Challenge Assumptions

Identify and challenge assumptions before making commitments.

### 4. Document Rationale

Decisions should be documented together with context and reasoning.

### 5. Prefer Reversible Decisions

When uncertainty is high, favor decisions that can be adjusted later.

### 6. Learning Beats Certainty

Organizations should optimize for learning rather than perfect predictions.

### 7. Culture Enables Decisions

Processes and frameworks help, but culture ultimately determines decision quality.
